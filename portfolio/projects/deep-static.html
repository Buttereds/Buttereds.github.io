DEEP

A narrative horror game about deep-sea isolation — and what silence does to a person.

SCREENSHOT / TRAILER

OVERVIEW

Deep Static is a slow-burn horror game set aboard a stricken deep-sea research station. There are no monsters. The horror comes from isolation, equipment failure, and the creeping uncertainty about whether the communications you're receiving are real.

The game has five distinct endings, each entirely driven by player choices — what to believe, who to trust, and whether to surface. There is no combat. There are no jump scares.

MY ROLE

Lead programmer on a three-person team (programmer, writer, sound designer). I built the dialogue and branching system from scratch in Unreal 5, implemented the procedural ambient audio engine, and handled all gameplay systems and UI engineering.

TECHNICAL HIGHLIGHTS

  • Custom dialogue engine with full branching, memory flags, and consequence tracking across 5 acts
  • Procedural ambient audio system built on MetaSounds — station sounds shift based on narrative state
  • Lumen-based lighting with dynamic material degradation as systems fail
  • Save system with 6 named slots and full narrative state serialisation
  • Localised into 4 languages at ship
